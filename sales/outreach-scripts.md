# Esmi Outreach Scripts

Copy-paste ready. Tuned for the beachhead: **South Florida home services (HVAC, plumbing,
roofing, electrical), bilingual (EN/ES)**. Swap the vertical words for med spa / dental as needed.

**The one mechanic that makes all of these work:** call the prospect's business yourself
*after 6pm* first. If it goes to voicemail (it usually does), you now have a true, specific
hook — "I just called you and got voicemail." That's a real lost lead, and it's undeniable.

---

## 1. Cold call — the after-hours opener (your best channel)

> "Hi, is this [Owner]? My name's [You] with Orchelix, here in [Boca/West Palm]. I'll be
> straight with you — I called your shop last night around 8pm and got your voicemail. I'm
> guessing I'm not the only one. Quick question: when a customer calls after hours or while
> your guys are on a job, what happens to that call?"

*(Let them answer. They'll admit it goes to voicemail / gets missed.)*

> "Right — and that customer just calls the next [plumber] on Google. I built an AI receptionist
> that answers every one of those calls 24/7, in English **and** Spanish, and books the job
> straight onto your calendar. I can have a version running on your business in a day. Can I
> show you — 10 minutes, and I'll have it answer a call live so you can try to stump it?"

**Goal of the call: book the demo. Don't sell on the call — sell in the demo.**

If "send me info": "Happy to — what's the best cell? I'll text you a 2-minute video of it
booking an appointment. But honestly it lands better if you just call it yourself — I'll text
you the number."

## 2. Voicemail (if they don't pick up)

> "Hi [Owner], it's [You] with Orchelix in [city]. I called your line after hours last night and
> got voicemail — wanted to flag that's probably costing you jobs. I've got an AI receptionist
> that answers 24/7 in English and Spanish and books appointments for you. I'll text you a
> 2-minute demo. Number's [your #]. Talk soon."

*(Then immediately text the Loom demo link + "this is the voicemail I left — here's the 2-min demo: [link]")*

## 3. Cold email sequence (4 touches over ~8 days)

**Email 1 — the miss (Day 1)**
Subject: `called you after hours last night`

> [Owner] — I called [Business] around 8pm yesterday and got voicemail.
>
> Not a complaint — just the thing that quietly costs home-services businesses the most: the
> after-hours / on-a-job calls that go to voicemail and then to your competitor.
>
> I run Orchelix. We put an AI receptionist (her name's Esmi) on your line that answers every
> call 24/7, in English and Spanish, and books the job onto your calendar.
>
> Want to hear her? I'll spin up a version trained on [Business] and text you the number so you
> can call it yourself. 10 minutes, no pitch.
>
> — [You], [phone]

**Email 2 — the math (Day 3)**
Subject: `the cost of a missed call`

> Quick math, [Owner]: if you miss ~5 calls a week and one would've been a real job at ~$[500],
> that's ~$2,000/month walking out the door. Esmi is a fraction of that and never misses a call.
>
> 2-minute demo here: [Loom link]. Or reply "call me" and I'll show you live.

**Email 3 — the Spanish angle (Day 5)**
Subject: `how many of your callers prefer Spanish?`

> In [South Florida], a big share of callers would rather book in Spanish — and most shops can't
> serve them after hours. Esmi does, fluently, automatically. That's market you're leaving to
> whoever picks up.
>
> Worth 10 minutes? [calendar link]

**Email 4 — the breakup (Day 8)**
Subject: `should I close your file?`

> [Owner] — haven't heard back, so I'll assume after-hours calls aren't a priority right now and
> stop reaching out. If that changes, the demo's here anytime: [link]. Either way, hope business
> is booming. — [You]

*(Breakup emails get the most replies. Don't skip it.)*

## 7. Agency / reseller partner pitch (your scale channel)

> and make your clients stickier: white-label our AI receptionist. You resell Esmi under your
> brand, we run the tech, you keep 25–30% recurring. One client is nice; your whole book is a
> real revenue line. Want to see how it works?"

*(Multi-tenant architecture means each of their clients is just a new tenant — no extra build.)*

---

## Rules that matter
- **Every script's goal is the demo, not the sale.** The product closes; you just get the at-bat.
- **Always offer the live call-it-yourself option.** It out-converts any deck.
- **Lead with the specific miss** ("I called you at 8pm") — generic pitches get ignored.
- **Spanish is a wedge, not a footnote** in South Florida. Name it early.
- Follow up 4–6 times. Most deals are in touches 3–5, which nobody sends.
